Frequently Asked Questions

BMI stands for Body Mass Index. It is a measurement of body fat based on height and weight.
BMI is calculated using the formula: BMI = weight (kg) / (height (m))^2.
A BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered healthy for most adults.
A high BMI may indicate obesity, which increases the risk of health problems, while a low BMI may suggest undernutrition.
